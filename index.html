About Ajmer

1. Ana Sagar Lake: This artificial lake built by Arnoraja Chauhan, is spread over the area of 13 kilometers. There is an island at the center of the lake which can be reached by boat. Walking along the Chowpatty and Jetty walkway provides a scenic view of the lake. Alongside the natural panorama of the place, the intriguing stories regarding it are also the center of interest for people.

2. Ajmer Sharif Dargah: One of the most famous sites of this place is the dargah of Moinuddin Chisti. Situated at the foot of Taragarh hill, the white marble buildings contain the domed tomb of the saint. It is a popular pilgrimage site with around 150,000 people visiting it every day.

3. Lake Foy Sagar: Another artificial lake situated in Ajmer was created under a famine relief project and is named after the man who created it. One of the popular tourist spots, it appears flat and provides a picturesque view of the neighboring Aravalli hills.

4. Nareli Jain Temple: Located on the outskirts of Ajmer, this Jain temple is situated in the Aravali mountain range. It is the seat of Jain deity Rishabhanath and is particularly crowded on occasions like Mahavir Jayanti. With further 23 miniature temples lined ahead of it, it is an exemplary mix of traditional and contemporary architectural style.

5. Adhai Din ka Jhopra: One of the oldest mosques in India and the Oldest surviving monument in Ajmer, this mosque was created in the time of Muhammad Ghori. A site maintained by the Archeological Survey of India, it is an early example of Indo-Islamic Architecture. It is known for the designs and artistry etched on its walls and ceilings and commended for the laborious accuracy of workmanship.

6. Ajmer Jain Temple: This Jain temple built in the 19th century is rich in its architecture and is dedicated to the Jain deity- Rishabhanath. Its main chamber is famous for its gold-plated wooden figures which are a depiction of several figures in the Jain religion.
